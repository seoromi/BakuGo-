About This Place

The Candy Mountains, also known as the Colored Mountains or Rainbow Mountains, are one of Azerbaijan's most spectacular geological wonders. Located in the Altƒ±aƒüac National Park near Quba, these extraordinary formations display vibrant stripes of red, yellow, purple, and orange that create a surreal, candy-like appearance across the mountainous landscape. This natural phenomenon results from millions of years of sedimentary rock formation and mineral deposits that have been exposed through erosion and geological activity.

The colorful striations are best visible during certain lighting conditions, particularly during golden hour when the sun's rays illuminate the mineral-rich layers in the rock formations. The area offers incredible photography opportunities and hiking trails that allow visitors to get close to these remarkable geological features. The contrast between the colorful mountains and the surrounding green valleys creates a breathtaking landscape that seems almost otherworldly, often compared to the famous rainbow mountains of China and Peru. Beyond their visual appeal, the Candy Mountains provide insight into Azerbaijan's geological history, with each colored layer representing different periods of sediment deposition and mineral formation over millions of years.
