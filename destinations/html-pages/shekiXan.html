Sheki Khan's Palace

Experience the architectural masterpiece of 18th-century Azerbaijan

About This Place

The Sheki Khan's Palace stands as one of Azerbaijan's most magnificent architectural treasures. Built in the 18th century, this summer residence of Sheki Khans showcases the pinnacle of Azerbaijani craftsmanship with its intricate frescoes, stunning stained glass windows (shebeke), and masterful woodwork that required no nails in its construction.

Every corner of this UNESCO World Heritage palace tells a story of Azerbaijan's rich cultural heritage. The building's facade is adorned with geometric patterns and floral motifs, while the interior features breathtaking ceiling paintings depicting hunting scenes, floral arrangements, and geometric designs that have remained vibrant for centuries. The palace represents the unique synthesis of Azerbaijani and Persian architectural traditions.

Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2019
  • Exquisite 18th-century Islamic architecture
  • Intricate frescoes and ceiling paintings
  • Stunning stained glass windows (shebeke)
  • Construction without using any nails
  • Rich collection of historical artifacts
  • Beautiful surrounding historic Sheki city
  • Traditional Azerbaijani craftsmanship showcase
