About This Place

The Ateshgah Fire Temple is one of Azerbaijan's most fascinating historical landmarks, located in the Surakhani suburb of Baku. Built in the 17th‚Äì18th centuries by Hindu and Zoroastrian communities, this pentagonal stone temple was a site of pilgrimage and worship for centuries, known for its eternal flames fueled by natural underground gas. The temple served as a spiritual center where fire-worshippers from India and Persia would journey to pay homage to the sacred flames.

With a central altar and surrounding cells for monks and pilgrims, Ateshgah reflects a unique blend of Persian, Indian, and Azerbaijani architectural influences. The complex features inscriptions in Sanskrit and Gurmukhi scripts, testament to its multicultural heritage. Although the natural gas flame ceased in the 20th century due to overexploitation of gas reserves, a simulated fire now burns in its place, preserving the temple's spiritual ambiance and allowing visitors to experience this remarkable piece of religious history. Designated as a State Historical-Architectural Reserve, Ateshgah remains a powerful symbol of Azerbaijan's multi-religious heritage and connection to ancient fire worship traditions.
