About This Place

Quba, nestled in the northeastern mountains of Azerbaijan, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, apple orchards, and rich cultural diversity. This picturesque region serves as the gateway to some of Azerbaijan's most spectacular mountain landscapes, including the famous village of Khinaliq ‚Äî one of the highest and most isolated settlements in the Caucasus. The area is celebrated for its pristine nature, traditional mountain lifestyle, and unique ethnic communities.

The region is famous for its apple production, earning it the nickname "Apple Land" of Azerbaijan. Beyond agriculture, Quba offers incredible hiking opportunities, visits to ancient mountain villages, and encounters with the Lezgian people who maintain their traditional customs and language. The nearby Afurja Waterfall, Tenghi Canyon, and the mysterious Khinaliq village provide visitors with a perfect combination of natural wonders and cultural experiences.
