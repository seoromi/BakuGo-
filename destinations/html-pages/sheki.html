About This Place

Sheki, one of Azerbaijan's most charming cities, sits nestled in the foothills of the Greater Caucasus Mountains. This ancient Silk Road settlement, dating back over 2,700 years, is renowned for its stunning Khan's Palace, traditional crafts, and rich cultural heritage. The city's crown jewel is the 18th-century Palace of Sheki Khans, a masterpiece of Azerbaijani architecture featuring intricate frescoes and stunning stained glass windows called "shebeke."

Beyond the palace, Sheki offers visitors cobblestone streets lined with traditional houses, ancient caravanserais, and workshops where artisans still practice centuries-old crafts like silk weaving and shebeke making. The city's location provides breathtaking mountain views and serves as a gateway to explore the region's natural beauty, including waterfalls, forests, and traditional mountain villages. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, Sheki represents the perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
