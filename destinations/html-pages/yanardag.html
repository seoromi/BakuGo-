Yanardag

Feel the heat of Azerbaijan's eternal flame at Yanar Dag ‚Äî a natural fire that has burned for centuries.

About This Place

Yanar Dag, meaning "Burning Mountain," is one of Azerbaijan's most awe-inspiring natural wonders. Located on the Absheron Peninsula, this hillside has been continuously ablaze for centuries due to natural gas seeping through the earth and igniting into flames. Unlike other mud volcanoes or fire temples, Yanar Dag's fire erupts directly from the ground and burns along a 10-meter stretch of hillside ‚Äî even in rain or snow. Revered since ancient times by fire-worshippers, this phenomenon contributed to Azerbaijan's nickname: "The Land of Fire."

A modern visitor center and interpretive museum explain the science, mythology, and cultural significance behind the flames, making Yanar Dag a must-see for those seeking both natural wonder and historical depth.

Highlights

  • Naturally burning hillside fueled by underground gas
  • Visible flames stretching over 10 meters
  • Key inspiration behind Azerbaijan's "Land of Fire" identity
  • Interpretive museum and visitor center
  • Popular evening and sunset viewing spot
  • Often visited with Ateshgah Fire Temple
Location
