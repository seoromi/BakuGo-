About This Place

Xinalig (also known as Khinaliq) is one of the highest and most isolated villages in Azerbaijan, perched at 2,350 meters in the Greater Caucasus Mountains. This remarkable settlement, with a history spanning over 5,000 years, is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the world. The village is home to the unique Khinaliq people, who have preserved their distinctive language, culture, and traditions despite centuries of isolation and harsh mountain conditions.

The village's dramatic setting offers breathtaking panoramic views of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, and deep valleys that stretch as far as the eye can see. Xinalig's traditional stone houses are built in a distinctive terraced style that follows the mountain's natural contours, creating a harmonious blend with the landscape. The Khinaliq language, spoken nowhere else in the world, and the community's ancient customs related to astronomy, agriculture, and mountain survival represent an invaluable cultural heritage. Reaching Xinalig requires a challenging but rewarding journey through some of Azerbaijan's most spectacular mountain scenery, making the village a destination for adventurous travelers seeking authentic cultural experiences and pristine nature.
