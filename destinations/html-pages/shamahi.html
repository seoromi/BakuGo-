About This Place

Shamakhi, one of Azerbaijan's most historically significant cities, served as the country's capital for over 800 years. Located on the ancient Silk Road, this cultural and spiritual center is renowned for its Islamic architecture, astronomical observatory, and rich traditions in poetry, music, and dance. The city's crown jewel is the magnificent Juma Mosque, considered one of the oldest and most important Islamic monuments in the Caucasus region.

Beyond its religious significance, Shamakhi is famous for the Shamakhi Astrophysical Observatory, perched high in the mountains and offering some of the clearest views of the night sky in the region. The area is also celebrated for its wine-making traditions dating back centuries, traditional dance culture immortalized in literature, and the nearby village of Lahij with its ancient crafts and cobblestone streets. Today, Shamakhi beautifully balances its historical heritage with modern development, offering visitors a unique glimpse into Azerbaijan's rich Islamic culture and scientific achievements.
