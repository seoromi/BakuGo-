Baku Old City

About This Place

Icherisheher, Baku's Old City, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that represents the historic core of Azerbaijan's capital. Enclosed within ancient defensive walls dating back to the 12th century, this remarkable medieval quarter houses over 50 historical monuments, including the iconic Maiden Tower and the magnificent Palace of the Shirvanshahs. Walking through its narrow cobblestone streets feels like traveling back in time through centuries of Azerbaijani history.

The Old City seamlessly blends residential life with historical preservation, where local families still live in traditional houses alongside world-class museums, art galleries, and restaurants. The area showcases various architectural influences from Persian, Arab, Ottoman, and Russian periods, creating a unique cultural mosaic. From ancient mosques and bathhouses to traditional carpet workshops and modern cafes, Icherisheher offers an authentic glimpse into both medieval and contemporary Azerbaijani culture. This living museum continues to be the cultural heart of Baku, hosting festivals, exhibitions, and cultural events while maintaining its historical authenticity and charm.

Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Maiden Tower (12th century)
  • Palace of the Shirvanshahs complex
  • Ancient defensive walls and gates
  • Traditional hammams (bathhouses)

  • Traditional carpet weaving workshops
  • Charming cobblestone streets and courtyards
  • Authentic restaurants serving traditional cuisine
