About This Place

Lahic is a remarkable mountain village that has preserved its medieval character and traditional crafts for over 1,000 years. Located in the Ismailli region, this ancient settlement is famous for its skilled coppersmiths, whose techniques have been passed down through generations unchanged since the Middle Ages. The village's cobblestone streets, traditional stone houses, and working craft workshops create an authentic medieval atmosphere that transports visitors back in time.

The village is renowned throughout the Caucasus for its exceptional metalwork, particularly copper vessels, weapons, and decorative items that showcase intricate traditional patterns and designs. Visitors can observe master craftsmen at work in their workshops, using the same tools and techniques their ancestors employed centuries ago. Beyond metalwork, Lahic also features traditional carpet weaving, woodworking, and other ancient crafts that represent the living heritage of Azerbaijan. The village's unique Tat language, distinct architecture, and cultural traditions have earned it recognition as a site of exceptional cultural value, making it one of Azerbaijan's most authentic and fascinating destinations for cultural tourism.
