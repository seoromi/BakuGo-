About This Place

The Maiden Tower (Qƒ±z Qalasƒ±) stands as Baku's most iconic landmark and Azerbaijan's most recognizable symbol. This mysterious 29-meter tall cylindrical tower, dating back to the 12th century, dominates the skyline of Baku's Old City and represents one of the finest examples of medieval Azerbaijani architecture. Despite extensive research, the tower's original purpose remains shrouded in mystery, with theories ranging from a Zoroastrian fire temple to a defensive fortification or astronomical observatory.

The tower's unique design features thick stone walls that taper as they rise, creating an almost impregnable structure that has withstood centuries of earthquakes, invasions, and time itself. Inside, visitors can climb the narrow spiral staircase through eight floors, each offering exhibits about the tower's history and archaeology. The climb culminates at the top with breathtaking panoramic views of the Caspian Sea, Baku's modern skyline, and the medieval Old City below. As a UNESCO World Heritage site and the inspiration for countless legends and stories, the Maiden Tower continues to captivate visitors with its enigmatic beauty and enduring mysteries.
